Case Quest

Outline:

Introducing Case Quest, a case study competition organized by BloomBox, the E-cell of KJSCE, presents an engaging challenge for participants. Comprising two rounds - Round 1 and Round 2 (Final Round) - this competition invites teams of 2-4 members to tackle provided problem statements with innovative solutions.

Round 1:

In Round 1, teams receive designated problem statements to analyze and provide solutions. They present their abstract solutions through an online platform, typically using Unstop. Submissions are evaluated based on the quality, feasibility, and innovation of the proposed solutions.

Rules (Round 1):

  1. Each team must consist of 2-4 members.
  2. Solutions must be presented in a PowerPoint format, limited to 5 slides including Introduction and Thank you Slide.
  3. The PowerPoint presentation must be uploaded in PDF format on Unstop.
  4. Any form of plagiarism will result in point deductions.
  5. Complete registration forms accurately without false information.
  6. A person can register as a part of only one team. Multiple registration will lead to disqualification.

Round 2:

Moving to Round 2, teams selected from Round 1 advance to the Final Round. Here, they are presented with a new set of problem statements and are required to create PowerPoint presentations to deliver their solutions in front of a panel of judges. Each presentation is allotted approximately 5 to 7 minutes. Following each presentation, there is an internal Q&A and group discussion session between the presenting team and other participating teams. Judges assess teams based on their presentation skills, the clarity of their responses during the Q&A session, and the depth of their understanding of the problem and proposed solution. Detailed rules will be provided to guide the proceedings.

Rules (Round 2):

  1. Selected teams will progress from Round 1 advance to Round 2.
  2. Teams create a PowerPoint presentation for new problem statements.
  3. Time limits to present and pitch the solution are limited to 5-7 minutes.
  4. Each team engages in an internal Q&A and group discussion session after their presentation.
  5. Participants must present original ideas; copying of solutions results in disqualification.
  6. Judges evaluate presentation skills and understanding of the problem and solution. Based on these points The decision made by the judges will be final.
